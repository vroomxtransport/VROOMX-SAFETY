The good news: CSA scores are not permanent. They are based on a rolling 24-month window, and every clean inspection pushes older violations out. Here are three concrete steps you can take right now:

1.  Challenge questionable violations with DataQ. Not every violation on your record is accurate. FMCSA's DataQs process lets you request a review of inspection data. A single successful challenge can drop a BASIC percentile by 5-15 points. Look for violations where driver or vehicle info was recorded incorrectly, or where the violation code does not match the narrative.
2.  Increase your clean inspection count. Each inspection without a violation dilutes your percentile. Encourage drivers to use PrePass and weigh station bypass programs, but also run regular internal pre-trip inspections. A clean Level 1 roadside inspection is one of the fastest ways to improve your Vehicle Maintenance and Unsafe Driving BASICs.
3.  Fix recurring violations at the source. Your top BASICs tell you exactly where to focus. If Unsafe Driving ({{unsafeDriving}}%) is elevated, invest in driver training on speeding and following distance. If HOS Compliance ({{hosCompliance}}%) is high, audit your ELD processes.

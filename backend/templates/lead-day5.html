The FMCSA Enforcement Escalation:

Step 1: Warning Letter

When BASICs exceed thresholds, FMCSA sends a warning letter. Most carriers ignore it. This is the window to act.

Step 2: Investigation / Compliance Review

An FMCSA investigator shows up and audits your driver files, maintenance records, HOS logs, and drug testing program. Average fines range from $1,000 to $16,000 per violation.

Step 3: Out-of-Service Order

In severe cases, FMCSA can issue an OOS order -- your trucks stop rolling, your revenue stops, and your authority is at risk. Reinstatement requires a safety audit with documented corrective action.

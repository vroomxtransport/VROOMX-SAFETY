What are the medical card requirements for CDL drivers?

CDL drivers must have a valid medical certificate (DOT medical card) to operate commercial vehicles. Here are the key requirements:

  • Valid for 2 years - Most certificates are valid for 24 months, though some conditions may require more frequent renewals
  • Must be on person - Drivers must carry their medical card while operating
  • Certified Medical Examiner - Exams must be performed by a licensed medical examiner listed on the FMCSA National Registry
  • Self-certification - Drivers must self-certify their operating status with their state DMV
